# Spec 037: Storage split

Collapse the dual PropertyGroup-vs-Custom-Property storage into one canonical home per field, by intent.

## Scope

- **Split PG-vs-CP storage by intent** - end the dual-fallback reads that keep spawning validator bugs (targets 1.0.0; depends on the format migration path; breaking).

## Study

### Surface notes

- The mirror is uniform and over-broad, and its two tables already disagree. `mirror_all_fields` flushes all 14 `OBJECT_MIRROR_MAP` rows ([`mirror.py:56-71`](../../apps/blender/core/mirror.py)) on every panel edit (`on_any_update`, [`_dynamic_items.py:66-75`](../../apps/blender/properties/_dynamic_items.py)) and again on every save ([`_handlers.py:107-118`](../../apps/blender/properties/_handlers.py)); `hydrate_object` re-applies a hand-maintained 11-row subset ([`hydrate.py:17-29`](../../apps/blender/core/_shared/hydrate.py)) on `load_post` plus a zero-interval deferred timer. `is_slot`, `slot_default`, and `is_outliner_favorite` are mirrored but never hydrated - the lockstep-table hazard the backlog cites (dedup finding N14) is live in the code. The locked option-A sketch classifies 12 of the 14 mirrored fields (7 PG-canonical, 4 CP-canonical, 1 drop) and leaves `is_slot` / `slot_default` unassigned, so the split opens with a classification decision, not just mechanics - both are writer-read, which under the sketch's "writer reads CP directly" rule points at CP-canonical.
- The dual-fallback read has one home and six consumers, so the read-side diff is bounded and enumerable. `read_field` / `read_bool_flag` ([`pg_cp_fallback.py:46-92`](../../apps/blender/core/_shared/pg_cp_fallback.py)) are consumed by the writer's element emission ([`sprites.py:72,160-176`](../../apps/blender/exporters/godot/writer/sprites.py)), slot emission ([`slots.py:43`](../../apps/blender/exporters/godot/writer/slots.py)), region resolution ([`region.py:40-65`](../../apps/blender/core/_shared/region.py)), the slot predicate ([`slot_emit.py:46`](../../apps/blender/core/slot/slot_emit.py)), and validation ([`_shared.py:21,33`](../../apps/blender/core/validation/_shared.py)); the export-correctness spec adds `active_slot.py` to that list before this lands, which is the right interim move - it concentrates every read in the one home this spec later retires. Panels keep drawing `obj.proscenio.*` either way; the panel-side change is not the reads but the CP-canonical group's PG fields becoming typed get/set proxy widgets, a known Blender footgun (`set=` suppresses `update=`, recursion guards) that replaces the mirror callbacks as the field-definition boilerplate in [`object_props.py`](../../apps/blender/properties/object_props.py).
- The headless path is the hidden cost center the backlog entry does not price. The fixture exporter loads the addon as a synthetic package and never calls `register()` ([`export_proscenio.py:30-53`](../../packages/fixtures/_shared/export_proscenio.py)), so every headless golden is produced from CP reads alone today; the fixture builders dual-write PG (guarded) plus CP (always) as a documented convention ([`README.md:138`](../../packages/fixtures/README.md)), and the PSD importer stamps CPs directly ([`planes.py:427-432`](../../apps/blender/importers/photoshop/planes.py)). Dropping the CP mirror for the PG-canonical group (`element_type`, the region fields, `material_isolated` - all writer-read) forces either property registration on the headless path or a re-target of every builder and the importer's stamping. The split reshapes the fixture convention and the PSD import leg, not just `apps/blender` internals; the `.proscenio` on disk is the one thing that does not change.
- The two animatable-storage idioms already disagree, and the split is what unifies them. Drive-from-Bone targets the nested PG path (`data_path = f"proscenio.{...}"`, [`driver.py:173`](../../apps/blender/operators/driver.py)) while slot keyframing already lives CP-side (`'["proscenio_slot_index"]'`, [`slot_animations.py:90`](../../apps/blender/exporters/godot/writer/slot_animations.py)). Blender cannot keyframe a field nested inside a PropertyGroup (T48975), so the CP-canonical move for `frame` and friends is forced by the platform, and the Drive-from-Bone operator's driver paths migrate with it - existing driven `.blend` files (the `mouth_drive` fixture among them) need the migrator to retarget or the docs to call the break.
- What gets deleted is the recurring bug generator; what gets added is a one-shot migrator. The mirror callbacks, the `save_pre` flush, the deferred timer, and the dual fallback all go; `hydrate.py` becomes a `load_post` migrator that lifts legacy CPs into the PG-canonical group and then deletes them, gated by a scene-PG version stamp so it runs once per file. CP deletion on real user files is the one data-destructive step in the addon's history, which is exactly why this rides the format-migration-path enabler (the [schema-expressiveness spec](../028-schema-expressiveness/STUDY.md)) and the 1.0.0 window rather than landing now. `on_depsgraph_update` (armature-pointer hygiene in the same handler module) is unrelated to the mirror and survives; scene-level props carry no CP mirror at all, so the split is Object-scope only.
- The pure parts are already unit-covered; the residual risk is GUI-shaped. [`test_mirror.py`](../../tests/test_mirror.py), [`test_pg_cp_fallback.py`](../../tests/test_pg_cp_fallback.py), and [`test_properties.py`](../../tests/test_properties.py) pin today's contract and get rewritten rather than extended, and the migrator is headless-testable against legacy-shaped mocks. What only a GUI session can confirm: undo behavior through the proxy widgets, disable -> save -> enable cycles (the resilience argument the split is built on), the migrator against real pre-split `.blend` files, and Drive-from-Bone retargeted onto CP paths.

### Assessment

| Item | Flow value | Test burden | Bug surface | Underuse risk | Verdict | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Split PG-vs-CP storage by intent | 4 | 4 | 3 | 1 | gate | ends the dual-read bug class for every field, but breaking + one data-destructive migration step; rides the migration-path enabler and the 1.0.0 window |

### Verdict summary

0 now, 0 defer, 1 gate, 0 drop.

The surface audit confirms both halves of the backlog's claim: the dual storage really is the recurring bug generator (the export-correctness spec's validator-pg-only defect is exactly this class), and the split really is a cross-cutting breaking change - six read consumers, the handler stack, the proxy-widget rewrite of the per-Object PG, the Drive-from-Bone driver paths, the PSD importer's stamping, every fixture builder's dual-write convention, and the headless-registration question, plus a once-per-file migrator that deletes user data. That is a medium-large refactor whose risk concentrates in GUI-only behaviors, so it spends the scarcest resource even though the wire format never moves. The timing verdict stands as designed: gate on the format-migration-path enabler landing in the schema-expressiveness spec and on the 1.0.0 window opening - block 1.0.0 on the split so the public surface ships the final storage contract, but keep it off the first tag. Until the gate opens, two cheap disciplines protect the eventual diff: new fields keep following the current uniform-mirror convention (never a third hybrid state), and every new field read keeps routing through `read_field` so the retirement stays one-home mechanical.
